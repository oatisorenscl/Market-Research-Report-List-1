<p><p>The animal-based compound feed market primarily consists of four types: mash, pellet, crumble, and other forms. Mash feed is a mixture of ground grains and additives, often used for poultry and pigs. Pelleted feed is processed into compact, uniform pellets, promoting easy consumption and reducing waste, commonly seen in livestock diets. Crumble feed is a coarsely ground version of pellets, facilitating easier intake for young animals. Other forms may include liquid feeds or block feeds, catering to specific nutritional needs and feeding practices.</p></p>
